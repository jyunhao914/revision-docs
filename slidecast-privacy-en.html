🔒 SlideCast Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2025

1Overview

SlideCast is an iOS app (iPhone and iPad) for recording educational lectures and presentations. We take your privacy seriously.

Core commitment: SlideCast does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers. All data remains on your device, and the app requires no internet connection to function.

2Device Permissions We Request

SlideCast requires the following device permissions to operate:

  • Camera: To record the instructor's face during a session. Video data is used solely for local recording and is never transmitted to any external service.
  • Microphone: To capture audio during recording. Audio data is used solely for local recording and is never transmitted externally.
  • Photo Library: To save finished lecture videos to your photo library. SlideCast does not read any of your existing photos.

All permissions can be revoked at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security.

3Data Collection

SlideCast does not collect any of the following:

  • Personal identifiers (name, email, accounts)
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, advertising IDs)
  • Usage analytics or crash reports
  • Location data
  • Slide content or recorded video
Apple App Store privacy label: "Data Not Collected"

4Local Data Storage

SlideCast only reads and writes the following data locally on your device:

  • App preferences (language, recording settings) — stored in UserDefaults, never leave the device
  • Temporary PDF rendering cache — stored in the app sandbox, automatically purged
  • Finished recording videos — saved to your photo library, managed by iOS

5Third-Party Services

SlideCast does not integrate any third-party analytics (e.g. Firebase, Amplitude, Sentry), advertising SDKs, or social login services. The app makes no outbound network connections.

6Children's Privacy

SlideCast is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from any age group.

7Policy Changes

If this policy is materially updated, we will revise the date at the top of this page and note the change in the App Store release notes.
